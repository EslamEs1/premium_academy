Getting started

Start here if you are still figuring out whether Sana Academy is the right fit, what the first step should be, or how the trial process works.

Best for

First-time families, adult learners, and trial planning.

Includes

Fit guidance, next-step clarity, and process expectations.

If you already know the subject, pace, and preferred teaching style, an individual teacher is often the best place to start. If you need more structure, clearer milestones, or a guided pathway, a program is usually the better fit. Sana Academy can help you decide before you commit.
The trial is designed to confirm fit, not just fill time. The teacher learns more about the student, demonstrates how they teach, and helps clarify the right next step in terms of pace, frequency, and learning focus.
In many cases, the academy can guide you toward a shortlist or next step within 24 hours of a strong inquiry. The exact timing depends on your subject, schedule preferences, and whether you need a teacher recommendation or program advice.

Category 02

Lessons & teachers

These answers focus on teacher fit, lesson experience, scheduling, and the kind of support learners can expect once they begin.

Covers

Teacher matching, lesson format, flexibility, and quality.

Designed for

Students, parents, and professionals evaluating fit.

If the fit is not right, Sana Academy can help rematch quickly. We treat teacher fit as a serious part of the learning process, so the goal is to protect momentum and move you toward a stronger option without unnecessary friction.
Most Sana Academy learning is delivered online because it offers better teacher access, more flexible scheduling, and easier continuity across time zones. Some teachers may offer alternative formats depending on location and subject.
Yes, within the limits of teacher availability and the learning plan. Sana Academy supports schedule coordination because real family and work calendars change. The aim is to preserve continuity without turning the process into chaos.
We look at both expertise and teaching quality. Strong subject knowledge matters, but so does the ability to explain clearly, challenge appropriately, build trust, and work well with the kind of learners the teacher serves best.
Category 03

Pricing & payments

Use this section for practical questions about how pricing is structured, when payment happens, and what kind of flexibility may be available.

Helpful for

Families comparing options and learners planning budgets.

Includes

Pricing logic, package context, and payment expectations.

Pricing depends on the teacher or program, the academic level, the subject complexity, and the intensity of support required. The goal is not to be vague, but to make sure the recommendation and pricing actually match the learning need.
Depending on the teacher or program, Sana Academy may offer session packages, installment structures, or guided payment arrangements. The exact option depends on the learning path, duration, and schedule intensity.
Payment timing depends on the structure you choose, but it is always clarified before you move forward. Sana Academy aims to remove confusion around pricing and next-step expectations before regular lessons begin.
Yes. If budget is part of the decision, it is better to say so early. The academy can often guide you toward the most sensible structure for your goal rather than forcing you into a recommendation that is not realistic to sustain.
